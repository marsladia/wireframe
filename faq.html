Straight answers

Questions, answered
honestly.

We'd rather tell you exactly what AlwayStart does — and what it doesn't — than win a sale that ends in a return.

It's the world's first automatic battery protector — a permanent, solid-state device that sits between your 12V battery and the vehicle. It continuously monitors the battery and isolates it before parasitic drain can deplete your starting charge. It is not a charger and not a jump starter; it holds no charge of its own.

Yes. After a one-time install, it monitors and isolates on its own — no cords, no app, no schedule, no reminders. The only manual step is a single press of ON when you return after the battery has been isolated.

A trickle charger needs an outlet and your memory. A jump pack only helps after you're already stranded — and is often dead itself. AlwayStart is proactive: it prevents the dead battery in the first place, automatically, with no cords or intervention.

Barely. Standby draw is around 5mA, and in UltraSleep mode it drops below 1 microamp — essentially invisible drain. It's engineered so the protector is never what kills your battery.

Gas and diesel vehicles with 12V lead-acid batteries — AGM, GEL, EFB, sealed, or flooded, top-post or side-terminal, most BCI group sizes. It is not compatible with EVs, hybrids, Start/Stop vehicles, lithium-battery systems, or 24V configurations.

It's a one-time setup with no special tools. You rotate the battery 180°, mount the unit using its integrated bracket, and connect the terminals. Most owners are done in minutes and never touch it again.

Yes — the custom solid-state switch is rated 1,200A peak and 200A continuous, with headroom for winches (up to ~8,000 lb), high-amp audio, supercharged builds, and overlanding accessory loads.

It's designed to meet or exceed MIL-STD-810H and IP68, uses AEC-qualified automotive-grade components and UL94 V-0 flame-resistant materials, and operates to 250°F. It's been through 11,000+ hours of endurance testing and 450+ days in the field.

The dual-battery kit is built for exactly that — it protects two 12V batteries and includes the wired remote. It ships March 2027.

Single units ship December 2026; the dual-battery kit ships March 2027. Shipping, taxes, and vehicle configuration are collected after the campaign.

Crowdfunding pricing is well below planned retail: $229 for the single unit (MSRP ~$349), $249 with the wired remote (MSRP ~$384), and $445 for the dual-battery kit (MSRP ~$628).
