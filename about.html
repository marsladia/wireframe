Behind the innovation

We refused to
license it away.

AlwayStart wasn't built to be a feature inside someone else's catalog. It was built to retire the dead battery for good.

The origin signal

A deal that fell apart for the right reason.

In 2025, the team entered licensing talks with a major battery-charger company. The deal collapsed once it became clear that AlwayStart wouldn't complement the incumbent's lineup — it would replace several of their product lines.

So the founders bootstrapped it to market themselves. The result is a genuinely new category: proactive, automatic battery protection that prevents the failure instead of reacting to it. The Kickstarter, "No More Dead Batteries," raised $251,020 from 891 backers against a $10,000 goal.
